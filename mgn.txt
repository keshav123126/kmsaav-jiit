<p>
<font size="5">
  <b style="color:green;">1. Objective:</b>
  <br>
  <span style="color:darkred;">
    Aims to provide employment to rural households by guaranteeing 100 days of wage employment annually to every rural household that demands work.  
    Seeks to enhance livelihood security and improve economic stability in rural areas.
  </span>
</p>

<p>
  <b style="color:green;">2. Scope:</b>
  <br>
  <span style="color:darkred;">
    Focuses on rural infrastructure development, including construction of roads, irrigation works, and water conservation projects.  
    Aims to create long-term sustainable livelihoods in rural areas through skill development and employment generation.
  </span>
</p>

<p>
  <b style="color:green;">3. Convergence:</b>
  <br>
  <span style="color:darkred;">
    Works in collaboration with state and local governments to implement rural development projects.  
    Encourages the integration of different developmental programs to maximize impact and ensure effective resource utilization.
  </span>
</p>

<p>
  <b style="color:green;">4. MNREGA Survey:</b>
  <br>
  <span style="color:darkred;">
    Conducts regular surveys to assess the employment demand, wage payments, and completion of public works in rural areas.  
    Uses data-driven strategies to identify gaps and areas that require more attention.
  </span>
</p>

<p>
  <b style="color:green;">5. Community Participation:</b>
  <br>
  <span style="color:darkred;">
    Encourages active participation of local communities in the planning and execution of projects.  
    Ensures transparency and accountability in the implementation of the scheme through social audits and local oversight.
  </span>
</p>

<p>
  <b style="color:green;">6. Vision:</b>
  <br>
  <span style="color:darkred;">
    Strives for sustainable rural development and the empowerment of marginalized communities through guaranteed employment.  
    Aims to ensure inclusive growth, poverty reduction, and rural prosperity in line with the broader objectives of national development.
  </span>
</p>

<p>
  <b style="color:green;">7. Key Features:</b>
  <br>
  <span style="color:darkred;">
    MNREGA focuses on demand-driven wage employment, meaning work is provided on request without discrimination.  
    Wages are paid directly to workers through bank or post office accounts to ensure transparency.  
    The program prioritizes women workers, mandating that at least one-third of beneficiaries are women.
  </span>
</p>

<p>
  <b style="color:green;">8. Implementation Strategies:</b>
  <br>
  <span style="color:darkred;">
    Gram Panchayats play a crucial role in planning, implementation, and monitoring at the grassroots level.  
    Focus is placed on decentralized planning to ensure alignment with local needs and priorities.  
    Emphasis is laid on skill development and capacity building to empower workers for future employment opportunities.
  </span>
</p>

<p>
  <b style="color:green;">9. Challenges:</b>
  <br>
  <span style="color:darkred;">
    Despite its success, MNREGA faces challenges such as delayed wage payments, fund allocation bottlenecks, and corruption.  
    Monitoring and evaluation mechanisms require strengthening to ensure the quality of work and reduce leakages.  
    Ensuring demand-based employment in remote and underserved areas remains an ongoing challenge.
  </span>
</p>

<p>
  <b style="color:green;">10. Achievements:</b>
  <br>
  <span style="color:darkred;">
    MNREGA has contributed significantly to rural development by providing livelihood security and reducing migration.  
    It has led to the creation of durable assets, such as roads, ponds, and irrigation facilities, benefiting the entire community.  
    The program has empowered rural women by providing them with economic independence and participation in local governance.
  </span>
</p>

<p>
  <b style="color:green;">11. Future Prospects:</b>
  <br>
  <span style="color:darkred;">
    MNREGA aims to expand its scope by integrating renewable energy and climate-resilient infrastructure projects.  
    Continued emphasis on skill development and capacity building will enable rural workers to transition to better employment opportunities.  
    Strengthening governance and technological interventions, such as geotagging of assets, will enhance transparency and accountability.
  </span>
</p>
